Sgt David E.Vincent- US. Army Special Agent

Sgt David E. Vincent has recently assumed duties as the second in command of the Maj of Staff of the U.S. Army, starting from August 9, 2021. Prior to this, he served as the Senior Staff agent of the U.S. Army. Born and raised in Fort Wayne, Indiana, Sgt. E.Vincent is a graduate of the U.S. Military Academy at West Point, New York. He holds a Master of Science in Aerospace Engineering from the Georgia Institute of Technology and was a National Security Fellow at Harvard University in 2005. Throughout his career, Sgt. David E.Vincent has held various assignments including a certified special gunnery sergeant of the 101st Airborne Division (Air Assault) and as the second in command of Combined Joint Task Force-101 during Operation Enduring Freedom. He has also served as the commander of 4th Brigade, 1st Cavalry Division during Operation Iraqi Freedom and held other significant roles such as U.S. Army deputy chief of staff, G-1, and chief of Legislative Liaison. As a highly skilled special Army aviator, Sgt. Vincent is qualified in the AH-64D Longbow Apache, OH-58 Kiowa Warrior, AH-6, AH-1 Cobra, and other aircraft. He has been recognized with numerous prestigious awards and decorations for his outstanding service. On a different note, Sgt. Vincent believes that the progress made by the U.S. in Iraq following the 2010 surge has been partially reversed, leading to the emergence of the Islamic State, also known as ISIS or ISIL.
